Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India

Here's a time line of Dr. A.P.J Abdul Kalam's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, India
  • 1954 - Graduated from University of Madras
  • 1955 - Moved to Madras to Study Aero Space Engineering in Madras Institute of Technology
  • 1960 - Graudated from Madras Institue of Technology and joined Aeronautical Development Establishment of DRDO as a Scientist
  • 1969 - Transferred to Indian Space Research Organisation as Project Director to develop Satellite Launch Vehicle(SLV)
  • 1970 - Directed Project Devil and Project Valiant to develop Ballisitc missiles
  • 1980 - Rohini satellite launched successfully with the help of SLV
  • 1992 - Became Chief Scientific Advisor for Prime Minister of India
  • 1998 - Developed a Coronary Stent along with Cardiologist Soma Raju
  • 2002 - Won Presidential Election and became President of India
  • 2012 - Designed a Rugged Tablet computer for Health care in Rural areas called Kalam-Raju Tablet
  • 2015 - Dies at the age of 83 while delivering a lecture in IIM Shillong
